# Recommended Devices for CareLoop — Larry Critzer's CHF Care

These devices integrate with or complement CareLoop. Listed in priority order based on clinical impact for CHF management.

---

## Priority 1 — Daily Weight (Most Critical for CHF)

### Withings Body Smart Scale (~$100)
The single most important CHF monitoring tool. Detects fluid retention before symptoms appear. Auto-syncs over Wi-Fi — no manual entry needed.

- **Amazon:** https://www.amazon.com/WITHINGS-Body-Smart-Percentage-Composition/dp/B0C3JNJPZ7
- **Withings direct:** https://www.withings.com/us/en/body-smart
- **Best Buy:** https://www.bestbuy.com/site/shop/withings-scales
- **Why this model:** Wi-Fi sync (no Bluetooth pairing needed each time), tracks weight + body composition, multiple user profiles, FDA-cleared, works with Android.
- **Clinical use:** CHF guidelines recommend daily weigh-ins at the same time each morning. A gain of 2+ lbs in 24 hours or 5+ lbs in a week = call the doctor immediately.

---

## Priority 2 — Blood Pressure Auto-Sync

### Withings BPM Connect (~$100–130)
Same Withings ecosystem as the scale — both sync to the same app. One-button operation, color-coded result display on the device itself (no phone needed to read results).

- **Amazon:** https://www.amazon.com/Withings-BPM-Connect-Pressure-Monitor/dp/B07SJV1HNR
- **Withings direct:** https://www.withings.com/us/en/bpm-connect
- **Why this model:** FDA-cleared, FSA/HSA eligible, syncs automatically, arm cuff (more accurate than wrist), same app as the scale.
- **Note:** If budget is a concern, the **Omron Platinum** (~$60) is the top-rated non-syncing option per Wirecutter and AARP.

---

## Priority 3 — AFib Detection (Critical Given His Diagnosis)

### KardiaMobile 6L by AliveCor (~$130–150)
FDA-cleared, medical-grade ECG. Place two thumbs on the pads for 30 seconds — detects AFib, bradycardia, tachycardia, and other arrhythmias. Cardiologists accept these readings directly.

- **Amazon:** https://www.amazon.com/s?k=KardiaMobile+6L
- **Best Buy:** https://www.bestbuy.com/product/alivecor-kardiamobile-6l-personal-ekg-monitor-black/J3RGS54SPF
- **Carewell (medical supplier):** https://www.carewell.com/product/alivecor-kardiamobile-6l/
- **Kardia direct:** https://kardia.com/products/kardiamobile-6l-max
- **Why 6L over standard:** 6-lead captures a much more complete picture than single-lead. The "6L" means it records leads I, II, III, aVL, aVR, aVF — the same leads a hospital 12-lead ECG uses for the limb leads.
- **Note:** Requires a subscription ($99/year or $10/month) for AI-interpreted readings. Without subscription, it still records and you can share the raw ECG with his cardiologist.

---

## Priority 4 — Oxygen Saturation (SpO2)

### Zacurate Pro Series 500DL Fingertip Pulse Oximeter (~$20–25)
Simple clip-on finger device. No app needed — just clip on and read. Your mom can enter the reading manually into CareLoop's daily check-in.

- **Amazon:** https://www.amazon.com/s?k=Zacurate+Pro+Series+500DL
- **Why this model:** Top-rated budget option, accurate, large display, includes alarm for low oxygen. Wirecutter and health.com both recommend it.
- **For auto-sync:** The **Withings ScanWatch** (~$250) includes continuous SpO2 monitoring and syncs automatically, but the manual oximeter is a perfectly good starting point.

---

## Optional — Smart Water Bottle (Fluid Tracking)

### HidrateSpark PRO 2 (~$60–85)
Glows and pulses to remind you to drink. Tracks fluid intake automatically via Bluetooth. Important note for CHF: your dad likely has a **fluid restriction** (not a "drink more" goal), so this would need to be configured as a daily limit tracker, not a hydration encourager.

- **Amazon:** https://www.amazon.com/Hidrate-Spark-PRO-Smart-Water-Bottle/dp/B0GBMTMQMS
- **HidrateSpark direct:** https://hidratespark.com
- **Important:** Discuss with his care team what his daily fluid limit is (often 1.5–2 liters/day for CHF). The bottle tracks toward a goal — set that goal to his limit, not a higher number.
